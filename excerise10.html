Captain America

Captain America, also known as Steve Rogers, is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Joe Simon and artist Jack Kirby, the character first appeared in Captain America Comics #1 in 1941 during the World War II era. Steve Rogers is a frail young man who undergoes a super-soldier experiment that enhances his strength, agility, and endurance to the peak of human potential.

Iron man

Iron Man, also known as Tony Stark, is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer-editor Stan Lee, artist Don Heck, and others, the character first appeared in Tales of Suspense #39 in 1963. Tony Stark is a billionaire industrialist, genius inventor, and playboy who creates a powered exoskeleton suit of armor to save his life after suffering a severe chest injury.

Thor

Thor is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Stan Lee, Larry Lieber, and artist Jack Kirby, the character first appeared in Journey into Mystery #83 in 1962. Thor is based on the Norse deity of the same name and is the Asgardian god of thunder. He wields the enchanted hammer Mjolnir, which grants him the ability to fly and control lightning, among other powers.

Hulk

The Hulk, also known as Bruce Banner, is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby, the character first appeared in The Incredible Hulk #1 in 1962. Bruce Banner is a physicist who, due to exposure to gamma radiation, transforms into the Hulk

Black Panther

Black Panther, also known as T'Challa, is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby, the character first appeared in Fantastic Four #52 in 1966. T'Challa is the king of the fictional African nation of Wakanda, which is technologically advanced due to its reserves of vibranium.

Black widow

Black Widow, also known as Natasha Romanoff, is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Stan Lee, artist Don Rico, and artist Don Heck, the character first appeared in Tales of Suspense #52 in 1964. Natasha Romanoff is a highly skilled spy, martial artist, and expert tactician
